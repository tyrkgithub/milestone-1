Mission

NoMoreAllMaleLineUps addresses inequalities within the music scene, of men (usually those who are cis, straight and white) being given opportunities over non-men.

×

No More All Male Lineups is a multigenre collective of female and non-binary DJs and live musicians, challenging gender imbalances across dance music spaces. Formed in response to the continued dominance of all-male lineups in Bristol and the UK more broadly, the collective organises events, takeovers, education and advocacy to create more inclusive dancefloors based on the understanding that a more diverse lineup produces more exciting musical journeys, richer experiences on the dance floor, and is more reflective of the community it is built for and built from.

Since its inception, No More All Male Lineups has curated and delivered fully inclusive club nights at iconic Bristol venues including Basement 45 and Loco Klub, packing out multi-room venues with femme and non-binary talent. This ethos extends beyond just music programming. They have collaborated with other non-male-led collectives such as Full Spectrum, WINK and Haven, and outsourced only to non-men, including for photographers, artists, stalls and décor; to ensure representation is built into every layer of an event, not treated as an afterthought.

Musically, No More All Male Lineups spans a broad and dynamic sound. The collective draws from a broad roster of DJs spanning house, disco, garage and 140, through drum & bass and jungle, up to techno, tek and donk, allowing lineups to be carefully shaped around the desired vibe, time slot and identity of each space. Whether delivering tightly focused genre takeovers or more eclectic journeys that build from deeper, chiller sounds into heavier energy, the collective works closely with promoters and programmers to ensure each booking fits seamlessly.

Alongside grassroots club nights, No More All Male Lineups has delivered takeovers across festivals and large-scale venues. These include appearances at major festivals such as Boardmasters; club series such as Cirque du Soul during Motion’s closing programme, and showcases with collectives including Space Camp, Kraft Tek, SIS:DEM.  We have taken over radio shows such as Void Radio, 85 Collective, SWU, Red Rebel and Bristol Community Radio. The collective has also supported high-profile artists at major venues, including Fish56Octagon at Document’s opening party, and holds ongoing residencies at well-loved Bristol venues such as the Rathouse, Basement 45, Love Inn and Café Kino.

A core part of the collective’s philosophy is skill-sharing and long-term artist development. We regularly run open decks sessions and music production workshops aimed at upskilling femme and non-binary artists, and lowering barriers to entry within DJing and production. They have also organised pay-what-you-can press photoshoots, enabling FLINTA DJs to access professional promo material that can otherwise be financially or logistically inaccessible. Beyond music, the collective has delivered talks and workshops on gender diversity in dance music, including sessions at festivals such as Nowadaze, and has contributed to panels and discussions for events such as International Women’s Day. No More All Male Lineups is also active across radio and media, having been interviewed by platforms such as Bristol247 to discuss gender representation, community organising and structural change within the dance music industry.

Across all of their work – from club nights and festivals to workshops, radio and advocacy — No More All Male Lineups centre community and collaboration, aiming not only to showcase underrepresented talent, but also to demonstrate that inclusive, representative lineups are creatively stronger, socially necessary, and essential to the future of dance music culture.
